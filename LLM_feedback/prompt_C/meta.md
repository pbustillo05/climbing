**Finger Injury Management for Rock Climbers – literature synthesis (2026)**

> Caveat: No randomized controlled trials (RCTs) exist for climbing-specific finger rehabilitation. Most protocols are extrapolated.

## 2. Narrative synthesis

### Sub-section 1: A2 Pulley Injuries

**Anatomy and mechanism**
- Five annular pulleys (A1-A5), A2 and A4 insert directly into bone and are least deformable.
- In climbers, middle finger (80/189) and ring finger (106/189) account for 98% of pulley injuries; A2 involved in 150/208 pulleys (72%), A4 in 33/208 (16%).
- Crimp grip places PIP 90-100° flexion, DIP hyperextension.
- Biomechanical modeling: crimp increases A2 force 31.5× and A4 force 3.9× versus open-hand.
- Recreational climbers generate 380-700 N on A2 (~54 kg).[Bioplausible]

**Grading (Schöffl 2003)**
- Grade I: pulley strain, no bowstringing
- Grade II: complete A4 or partial A2, mild bowstringing
- Grade III: complete A2
- Grade IV: multiple ruptures (A2/A3, A2/A3/A4) ± lumbrical/collateral injury.[Bioplausible]

**Diagnosis**
- Clinical: pain on resisted PIP flexion, palpable tenderness over volar proximal phalanx, occasional pop. Bowstringing often absent acutely.
- Imaging: ultrasound and MRI recommended to confirm grade.
- Ultrasound is de facto gold standard but lacks consensus: complete A2 rupture TBD thresholds range 1.9-5.1 mm across 7 studies; A4 1.8-3.1 mm.
- High-frequency US accuracy for flexor tendon rupture 92.86% in hand trauma series.[Bioplausible]

**Conservative management (Grades I-II, most III)**
- Schöffl protocol: Grade I: 0d immobilization, functional therapy 2-4wk, tape protection. Grade II: 10d immobilization, then 2-4wk functional. Grade III: 14d immobilization, then up to 4wk functional, thermoplastic ring. [Bioplausible: case series, no RCT]
- Outcomes: 80 conservatively treated grades I-III returned to prior UIAA level; 7 delayed by tenosynovitis.
- Return-to-loading timeline: no climbing RCT defines safe onset. [Folklore: community practice]
- H-taping: Schöffl 2007 controlled trial (n=8 US, n=12 strength) showed H-tape reduced tendon-bone distance 16% versus no tape; other tapes ineffective. Crimp strength improved 13% with H-tape, no change in open-hand hang. [Bioplausible: small n, no CI]
- Load-managed rehabilitation rationale extrapolated from tendinopathy literature:
  - Alfredson 1998: 15/15 chronic Achilles patients returned to running after 12wk heavy eccentric; strength normalized. [Evidence-backed: prospective cohort]
  - Meta-analysis: eccentric exercise reduced pain MD -1.21 (95% CI -2.72 to -0.30) versus other exercise. [Evidence-backed]
  - Cook & Purdam 2009 continuum model proposes load drives tendon adaptation through reactive, dysrepair, degenerative stages (DOI 10.1136/bjsm.2008.051193). [Bioplausible: mechanistic]
  - Rio 2015: single bout isometric leg extension in patellar tendinopathy reduced pain 6.8/10 immediately (7.0±2.04 to 0.17±0.41) sustained 45min, MVIC ↑18.7±7.8%. [Evidence-backed: small RCT]
- Extrapolation to pulleys remains [Bioplausible: no direct pulley RCT].[Bioplausible]

**Surgical management (Grades III-IV)**
- Indications: complete A2 with bowstringing >2mm, multiple pulleys, failed conservative, or acute high-demand athlete.
- Techniques: Lister extensor retinaculum, Kleinert/Weilby palmaris graft, loop-and-a-half.
- Outcomes: Arora 2007 (n=23) excellent/good Buck-Gramcko in 21/23, no major complications. Bouyer 2016 (n=38) return to prior level correlated with post-op US E-space <2mm (70% vs 25%). [Bioplausible: retrospective case series]
- Return-to-climbing: case series of direct suture in elite climbers reported mean 5.8 months to pre-injury level.[Bioplausible]

**Load-managed hangboard rehabilitation during recovery**
- Ehiogu et al 2023 clinical commentary states rehabilitation should be grounded in progressive loading to improve morphological properties, not time alone.
- Tyler Nelson protocol (widely cited): start pain-free isometric hangs at 20-30% bodyweight, open-hand, 10s x5, 2-3x/week; progress load 5-10% weekly if pain ≤2/10; introduce half-crimp week 6-8; full crimp avoided until week 12. [Folklore: Tyler Nelson, The Climbing Doctor]
- No RCT validates thresholds or superiority over rest. [Bioplausible gap][Bioplausible]

### Sub-section 2: Flexor Tendon Strains
- Anatomy: FDP and FDS share A2 sheath; strain typically at musculotendinous junction from eccentric overload.
- Mechanism: similar to pulley but pain proximal to PIP, no bowstringing.
- Grading: Grade I stretch, II partial tear, III complete – mirrors muscle strain scales, not validated for fingers. [Folklore: physiotherapy consensus]
- Management: principles overlap pulley rehab – brief immobilization 3-7d, then progressive isometric loading, avoid crimp 4-6wk. No climbing RCT.[Bioplausible]

### Sub-section 3: Skin Management

**Flappers**
- Acute: trim loose skin with sterile scissors, clean, apply petrolatum or hydrocolloid, tape with non-adhesive pad. [Folklore: Anderson brothers, Lattice Training]
- No RCT comparing dressings. [Bioplausible gap]
- Return to climbing: community practice 3-7 days with taping if pain-free pinch.[Folklore]

**Chronic skin thinning**
- Mechanism: repeated friction on small holds reduces stratum corneum.
- Interventions: filing calluses, moisturizing, sanding holds, humidity control 40-60%. No peer-reviewed trials. [Folklore: Dave MacLeod, ClimbSkin][Bioplausible]

**Collagen supplementation**
- Burn RCT: hydrolyzed collagen improved wound healing and pre-albumin, hospital stay reduced ~30% versus control. [Evidence-backed: pilot RCT]
- Meta-analysis 11 RCTs: collagen dressings increased healing rate RR 1.53, reduced recurrence RR 0.47. [Evidence-backed]
- Oral bioactive collagen peptides 12wk improved skin hydration, elasticity, sustained 4wk washout. [Evidence-backed]
- Direct relevance to climbing flappers untested. [Bioplausible extrapolation]

### Sub-section 4: General Injury Prevention Principles
- Warm-up: Schweizer 2001 found 100 progressive moves increased physiologic bowstring, implying increased pulley compliance. No RCT shows injury reduction.
- Load management: 10% weekly increase rule originates from running literature, no climbing validation. [Folklore: training community]
- Experience-dependent risk: Sjöman 2023 (n=434) found finger strength training negatively associated with severe pulley injury in climbers with ≥6yr experience (χ²=4.57, p=0.03), but positively associated in <6yr climbers climbing ≥7a (χ²=4.61, p=0.03). [Evidence-backed: cross-sectional][Bioplausible]

## 3. Open questions
1. What load magnitude and frequency of hangboard isometrics maximizes pulley collagen synthesis without re-rupture after grade II-III A2 tear? No dose-response RCT exists.
2. Does H-taping alter long-term healing or only acute tendon-bone distance? Current data are acute biomechanical only.
3. Can the Cook-Purdam continuum be validated for pulleys using serial ultrasound elastography during rehab?
4. Does oral collagen peptide supplementation accelerate return-to-climbing after flapper or pulley injury versus placebo in climbers?
5. What ultrasound TBD cutoff best predicts clinical failure of conservative treatment and need for surgery, given current range 1.9-5.1 mm?
