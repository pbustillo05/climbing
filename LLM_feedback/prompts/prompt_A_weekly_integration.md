# Prompt A — Weekly Training Integration for Rock Climbers

## Context and purpose

I am writing a scientific literature review on supplemental finger-strength training for rock climbing. I need you to research and draft content for a section on **how to integrate multiple training modalities — limit bouldering, Moonboard sessions, hangboard sessions, and top-rope/lead climbing — into a 7-day weekly schedule** for intermediate-to-advanced climbers (approximately V6–V9 / 5.12–5.13c redpoint level).

## Evidence classification framework

Every claim in your response must be labelled with one of three tiers:

- **Evidence-backed**: supported by peer-reviewed intervention data with adequate controls. Cite specific studies (author, year, DOI if known). Report effect size and 95% CI where available.
- **Bioplausible**: mechanistically plausible from physiology or biomechanics, but no direct controlled intervention evidence exists. State the mechanism and the gap explicitly.
- **Folklore**: community consensus, coaching heuristic, or practitioner claim without empirical support. Name the source explicitly (e.g., `r/climbharder`, Lattice Training blog, specific coaching book with edition, YouTube channel).

Do not promote a Folklore claim to Bioplausible without stating a mechanism. Do not promote a Bioplausible claim to Evidence-backed without a cited RCT or systematic review.

## Writing style

Dense, precise, information-complete. Every sentence must carry a fact, a number, a constraint, or a classification. No filler, no transitional summaries. Numbers over words: write `48 h`, `≥80% MFS`, `SDM = 0.41 (95% CI 0.03–0.80)`, not "several hours" or "high intensity." Flag contested or unverified claims explicitly rather than softening them.

### 2. Narrative synthesis

Cover the following sub-topics. For each, state the evidence tier first, then the content:

**a) Fatigue interference between modalities**
- How does limit bouldering (near-maximal finger loading, high CNS demand) interfere with hangboard adaptation if placed too close in the same microcycle?
- What does the concurrent-training literature (strength + endurance interference) say, and does it apply to climbing's unique finger-loading profile?
- Minimum recovery time between a high-intensity finger session (hangboard or limit bouldering) and the next finger-intensive session: what does evidence say vs. what does practitioner consensus say?

**b) Session sequencing rules**
- Should hangboard training precede or follow a projecting/limit bouldering session? What is the rationale?
- Where do Moonboard sessions (high-intensity, system-board style, finger-intensive) fit relative to hangboard days?
- Where do top-rope/lead endurance sessions fit? What is their fatigue profile relative to bouldering and hangboarding?
- Can hangboard and limit bouldering be done on the same day? If so, in what order and with what inter-session gap?

**c) Example weekly structures**
Provide 2–3 concrete weekly templates for a climber training 4–5 days per week. Label each day. Include: which modality, approximate duration/volume, intensity descriptor (max effort / submaximal / technique). Flag which templates are evidence-derived vs. folklore-derived.

Example format:
- Mon: Hangboard max-hangs (85% MFS, 3×5 sets, 20 min) [Evidence-backed: López-Rivera 2019]
- Tue: Rest
- Wed: Limit bouldering projecting (90–100% effort, 2–3 h) [Folklore: r/climbharder wiki]
- Thu: Active recovery / technique laps [Bioplausible: low-load active recovery rationale]
- Fri: Moonboard session (benchmark problems, 70–85% effort, 90 min) [Folklore: Moonboard training guides]
- Sat: Top-rope/lead endurance (routes at 60–75% max effort, 2 h) [Bioplausible]
- Sun: Rest

**d) Deload triggers**
- When should a climber reduce volume/intensity across all modalities simultaneously?
- Are there performance markers or subjective readiness signals that predict overreaching before it becomes overtraining?

### 3. Open questions

List 3–5 specific questions the literature cannot yet answer about weekly integration for climbers.
