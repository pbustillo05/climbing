# Prompt F — Equipment ROI for Climbing Training Tools

## Context and purpose

I am writing a scientific literature review on training for rock climbing. I need to build a section that helps a climber (intermediate-to-advanced: V6–V9 / 5.12–5.13c redpoint) make evidence-informed decisions about purchasing and prioritising training equipment. The section should rank equipment by bang-for-buck — combining outcome relevance, cost, and evidence quality — so a reader can make budget-prioritised decisions with explicit evidence context.

**Your task**: produce an exhaustive research and community-consensus synthesis on climbing training equipment. Use your training data AND active web searches across PubMed, Google Scholar, Lattice Training, Hooper's Beta, r/climbharder, climbing equipment vendor sites, YouTube training channels, and any other relevant sources. Be exhaustive. Cover every category of training tool used in modern climbing training. Flag every claim with its evidence tier and source.

## Citation format

- Scientific papers: `[DOI | Authors | Title]`
  - Example: `[10.5114/biolsport.2023.113295 | Stien et al. 2023 | Meta-analysis of hangboard training]`
  - If DOI unverified: `[DOI unverified | Authors | Title | Journal, year]`
- Other resources: `[URL or source description]`
  - Example: `[https://latticetraining.com/... | Lattice Training blog]` or `[r/climbharder wiki — Equipment FAQ]`
- Every claim must cite at least one source. No uncited assertions.

## Evidence classification (apply to every claim)

- **Evidence-backed**: peer-reviewed intervention data with adequate controls. Cite studies, report effect size and 95% CI where available.
- **Bioplausible**: mechanistically plausible, no direct controlled intervention evidence. State mechanism and gap.
- **Folklore**: community consensus or practitioner claim without empirical support. Name source explicitly.

## Writing style

Dense, precise, information-complete. Numbers over words. Flag contested claims explicitly rather than softening them.

## Output is freeform — focus entirely on content

Plain prose and lists, structured by equipment category. Maximum information density and citation coverage. No LaTeX, no formatting polish.

## Equipment categories to cover (be exhaustive within each)

### 1. Hangboards / Fingerboards

**Evidence base:**
- What does the RCT and systematic review literature say about hangboard training efficacy? (Stien et al. 2023, Lopes et al., López-Rivera 2019, Devise et al. 2022, and anything else)
- Protocols studied: max-hangs, repeaters, density blocks — what outcomes were measured, what effect sizes found
- Which hangboard features matter for training outcomes: edge depth (18–20mm vs. other), surface friction, hold variety, sloper/pinch options
- Any evidence comparing specific hangboard brands/models on training outcomes (unlikely, but check)

**Product landscape:**
- Metolius: Simulator, Project Board
- Beastmaker: 1000, 2000
- Tension: Grindstone, Wood Grips variants
- Lattice: Lattice Board, Rung Rungs
- Transgression / other boutique boards
- DIY wood options (cost vs. quality tradeoff)
- Key feature differences and who each suits (beginner vs. advanced, pulleys vs. tendons)

**Community consensus and folklore:**
- Which boards does the climbing community (r/climbharder, YouTube coaches, Lattice, Power Company Climbing) actually recommend and why, independently of any evidence base?
- Community folklore around edge texture (smooth wood vs. rough resin), edge rounding, and how these affect skin and pulleys — explicitly name sources
- Any coaching or community heuristics about who should NOT use a hangboard yet (strength thresholds, years climbing), and where these heuristics come from

**ROI analysis:**
- Cost range, space requirement, installation complexity
- What training goals it directly serves vs. what it cannot provide
- Bang-for-buck ranking with rationale

### 2. Force measurement devices (isometric testing tools)

**Evidence base:**
- What is the evidence for using force measurement to guide training (critical force concept, W', force asymmetry)?
- Does force-guided training outperform non-guided periodisation in climbers?
- Validity and reliability data for specific devices

**Product landscape:**
- Tindeq Progressor: protocol compatibility, software ecosystem, force-velocity profiling capability
- Lattice Entralpi (or equivalent load cells with apps)
- Generic force gauges (Baseline, Pesola) used in research contexts
- DIY load-cell setups
- Feature differences: BLE connectivity, app data export, sampling rate, accuracy specifications

**Community consensus and folklore:**
- What does the community (r/climbharder, coaching blogs, Tindeq user forums) say about who benefits from force measurement vs. who is "not ready" for it?
- Community-derived benchmarks: what scores on Tindeq or Lattice testing does the community consider "good for V8" or "elite"? Name every source explicitly
- Practitioner opinions on whether self-coached climbers can extract actionable data without a coach — name sources

**ROI analysis:**
- Who benefits most (intermediate testing vs. advanced periodisation)
- Cost vs. information value
- Whether a climber without coaching support can extract meaningful data

### 3. Campus boards

**Evidence base:**
- Any RCT or controlled study on campus board training for climbing performance
- Injury risk data: estimated incidence of campus board-related injuries (pulleys, elbows)
- Appropriate populations: consensus on minimum finger strength requirements before campus training
- Rung spacing standards: standard vs. modified (Moon, Lattice)

**Folklore and practitioner consensus:**
- Community and coaching consensus on campus training utility
- Progression models: 1-5-9, laddering, matching — sources
- When campus training is appropriate vs. counterproductive

**ROI analysis:**
- Space and cost requirements
- Injury risk weight in ROI calculation
- Bang-for-buck vs. other tools

### 4. System boards and woody training boards (Moonboard, Kilter, Tension Board, spray wall)

**Evidence base:**
- Any controlled study on system board training vs. general climbing for performance
- Kilter/Tension/Moonboard as training stimuli vs. performance testing tools: distinction

**Practitioner and community consensus:**
- r/climbharder, Moonboard community, coach perspectives on system board utility
- Benchmark problem sets: standardisation advantages
- Limitations: lack of volume control, injury profile

**ROI analysis:**
- Cost (commercial gym access vs. home installation)
- Who benefits (intermediate vs. advanced)
- Unique value relative to hangboard + limit bouldering

### 5. Weighted vests and resistance training equipment

**Evidence base:**
- RCTs on weighted pull-ups, lock-off training, antagonist work in climbers (any studies)
- Transfer to climbing performance: what does evidence say

**Product landscape:**
- Commercial weighted vests (range, fit quality)
- Resistance bands for antagonist and shoulder work
- Pulley systems for assisted/resisted climbing movements

**ROI analysis:**
- Evidence quality for the training stimulus provided
- Cost relative to value

### 6. Core and antagonist training equipment

**Evidence base:**
- Evidence on antagonist training reducing injury incidence in climbers (any studies)
- Evidence on core strength and climbing performance correlation

**Product landscape:**
- Gymnastic rings
- TRX / suspension trainers
- Parallettes
- Standard gym access vs. home setup

**ROI analysis:**
- Cost, versatility, space requirement
- Bang-for-buck compared to other tools

### 7. Recovery and monitoring tools

**Evidence base:**
- HRV monitors: evidence on HRV-guided training (Kiviniemi et al. 2007 and others) — does HRV-guidance improve outcomes?
- Wearable training load monitors for climbing (any data)
- Compression sleeves, tape, foam rollers for climbing-specific recovery: evidence review

**Product landscape:**
- HRV apps: HRV4Training, Elite HRV, Polar H10 setup
- Garmin/WHOOP/Oura for recovery monitoring
- Tape: rigid vs. elastic; H-tape protocol for pulleys
- Compression garments: specific studies on forearm/hand

**ROI analysis:**
- Which recovery tools have the best evidence-to-cost ratio
- Which are primarily folklore-based
