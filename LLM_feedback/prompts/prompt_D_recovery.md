# Prompt D — Recovery for Rock Climbers

## Context and purpose

I am writing a scientific literature review on supplemental training for rock climbing. I need you to draft content for a section on **recovery modalities**, covering sleep, HRV monitoring, contrast therapy, soft-tissue work, active recovery, and nutrition timing — all filtered specifically for their relevance to **finger, forearm, and connective tissue recovery** in rock climbers.

Climbing has a distinctive fatigue profile: high-intensity isometric finger loading, substantial forearm oxidative demand, and connective tissue (tendon/pulley) stress that recovers on a different timeline (days to weeks) than muscle (24–72 h). Recovery modalities must be assessed against this specific profile.

## Evidence classification framework

- **Evidence-backed**: peer-reviewed intervention evidence with adequate controls. Cite study (author, year, DOI). Report effect size and 95% CI. Note whether the outcome is surrogate (e.g., blood markers, dynamometric) or functional (e.g., climbing performance, grip strength).
- **Bioplausible**: mechanistically plausible, no direct controlled climbing evidence. State the mechanism and the gap.
- **Folklore**: community/coach consensus. Name the source explicitly.

## Writing style

Dense, precise, information-complete. Numbers over words. Flag every tier explicitly. Flag surrogate vs. functional outcomes.

## What I need

### Sub-section 1: Sleep

- RCT or cohort evidence on sleep duration/quality and athletic performance (grip strength, reaction time, power output). Effect sizes?
- Is there climbing-specific sleep research?
- Sleep deprivation effects on connective tissue — any evidence? (collagen synthesis is linked to growth hormone release during slow-wave sleep)
- Practical recommendations: duration, timing, sleep hygiene — Evidence-backed or Folklore?

### Sub-section 2: Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Monitoring

- What is HRV and what does it measure (autonomic recovery proxy)?
- Evidence for HRV-guided training vs. fixed-schedule training: any RCTs? Effect on performance outcomes?
- HRV thresholds for "ready to train" decisions — Evidence-backed or Folklore?
- Tools (Whoop, Garmin, HRV4Training app) — are their algorithms validated?
- Relevance specifically for connective tissue readiness: does HRV capture tendon/pulley fatigue, or only neuromuscular/cardiovascular?

### Sub-section 3: Contrast Therapy (Cold/Hot Alternation)

- Mechanism: alternating vasoconstriction/vasodilation to enhance metabolic waste clearance and blood flow
- RCT evidence for contrast therapy vs. passive rest for muscle recovery (performance, DOMS, lactate clearance). Report effect sizes.
- Any evidence for connective tissue recovery specifically?
- Ice/cold-water immersion (CWI) separately: evidence for and against (blunted hypertrophic adaptation with CWI post-strength training — relevant for climbers doing hangboard work)
- Practical protocol if evidence-supported: temperature, duration, cycles

### Sub-section 4: Soft-Tissue Work (Massage, Foam Rolling)

- RCT evidence for massage or foam rolling on forearm recovery after isometric exercise or climbing
- Effect sizes for DOMS reduction, ROM restoration, performance recovery
- Timing: immediate post-session vs. next-day — any difference?
- Climbing-specific tools (e.g., forearm massage, finger flexor release) — Evidence-backed or Folklore?

### Sub-section 5: Active Recovery Sessions

- Evidence for low-intensity active recovery vs. passive rest for improving subsequent performance
- What constitutes "active recovery" for climbers? (easy laps on jugs, easy bouldering, antagonist training, yoga)
- Forearm blood flow and lactate clearance with light movement — mechanistic evidence?

### Sub-section 6: Nutrition Timing for Connective Tissue

- Post-exercise protein: dose (g), type (whey, casein, collagen), and timing for muscle protein synthesis — well-established in the literature; summarize briefly and note this is mostly muscle-focused
- Connective tissue-specific: is there evidence that protein timing affects tendon collagen synthesis differently from muscle? Reference the Shaw et al. (2017) pre-exercise collagen + vitamin C protocol here.
- Carbohydrate and total energy availability: evidence for underfueling impairing tendon health or climbing recovery?
