### 1. Add the missing collagen-peptide RCTs and score them for climbing relevance (0 = irrelevant, 10 = direct grade outcome)

Your table stops at Shaw (gelatin) and Praet (pilot). Since 2022 four independent RCTs have tested **hydrolyzed collagen peptides** on tendon structure:

- **[10.1111/sms.14164 | Jerger S et al. | Effects of specific collagen peptide supplementation combined with resistance training on Achilles tendon properties]** – 14 wk, 5 g/d, +11% Achilles CSA vs +4.7% placebo. Quality: moderate, n=40. **Climbing relevance 3/10** (large tendon, no finger data).
- **[10.1080/17461391.2023.2232758 | Jerger S et al. | Specific collagen peptides increase adaptions of patellar tendon morphology following 14-weeks of high-load resistance training]** – same dose, patellar CSA gains at 60–70% length. **Relevance 3/10**.
- **[10.3389/fphys.2023.1089971 | Lee J et al. | Collagen supplementation augments changes in patellar tendon properties in female soccer players]** – 10 wk, 30 g hydrolysate + vit C, stiffness ↑. **Relevance 4/10** (athletes, high load).
- **[10.1113/EP092106 | Lee J et al. | High-intensity resistance training and collagen supplementation improve patellar tendon adaptations in professional female soccer athletes]** – replication in pros. **Relevance 4/10**.
- **[10.1002/ejsc.12281 | Nulty CD et al. | Hydrolysed Collagen Supplementation Enhances Patellar Tendon Adaptations to 12 Weeks’ Resistance Training in Middle-Aged Men]** – 30 g post-training, CSA +6.8 mm². **Relevance 3/10**.
- **[10.1249/MSS.0000003814 | Miyamoto N et al. | Collagen Peptide Supplementation Enhances Muscle-Tendon Stiffness and Explosive Strength]** – 16 wk, 10 g/d, Achilles stiffness ↑. **Relevance 3/10**.

### 2. Separate gelatin from hydrolyzed peptides – folklore conflates them

Climbers on TrainingBeta and Reddit routinely say “gelatin = collagen” [https://trainingforclimbing.com/collagen-for-climbers-latest-research-supports-benefits/]. Biologically they differ: gelatin gels, peptides dissolve cold, peptides have higher digestibility. Your text lumps them in one row. Split them and note that all recent positive structural data use peptides, not kitchen gelatin.
