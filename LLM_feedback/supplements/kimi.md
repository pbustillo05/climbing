### Summary of changes made

**Collagen (§3)**
- Added a new subsection *Recent Systematic and Trial Evidence (2023–2026)* covering:
  - **Boldt et al. 2023** – exercise + collagen improves Achilles pain/structure but stiffness effects are inconsistent
  - **Balshaw et al. 2023** – important null RCT: no tendon remodeling benefit in healthy young men
  - **Miyamoto et al. 2025** – collagen alone may alter muscle-tendon stiffness in untrained males
- Softened the vitamin-C gap language: changed "critical gap" → "evidence-aligned gap" and "single highest-priority protocol modification" → "most evidence-aligned modification"
- Added explicit caveat that *no factorial RCT has proven vitamin C is strictly required in vitamin-C-replete athletes*
- Updated the pulley-remodeling bioplausible box to acknowledge Balshaw 2023 as a counterweight

**Creatine (§4)**
- Added **Wang et al. 2024** meta-analysis (upper- and lower-body strength gains, stronger in males)
- Elevated the **body-mass penalty** as the *central practical caveat* and separated it clearly from strength/power benefits
- Converted the "too heavy to climb" claim from `\toverify` to `\folkloreverified`, framing it as a legitimate practical concern rather than a proven contraindication

**Omega-3 (§5)**
- Sharpened practical framing: recovery/anti-inflammatory *hypothesis*, not a proven pulley-repair intervention

**Magnesium (§6)**
- Tightened scope to **deficiency correction, possible sleep support, weak cramp evidence**
- Added **Garrison et al. 2020 Cochrane review** (magnesium unlikely to prevent idiopathic cramps)
- Converted forearm-pump/cramp claims to `\folkloreverified` and explicitly labeled them unsupported in replete climbers

**Beta-alanine (§7)**
- Clearly distinguished *campus/easy-traverse* benefits from *route/redpoint* performance
- Noted hard-traverse negative result and limited applicability to **~1–4 min high-intensity efforts**, not all pump

**Caffeine (§8)**
- Kept language **dose-specific (3 mg/kg)** and **sample-size-specific (n=13)**
- Emphasised that subjective alertness increased while performance proxies were null

**What the Literature Cannot Currently Resolve (§9)**
- Added an **overarching gap** paragraph as the peer reviewer requested: the field still lacks climbing-specific RCTs with hard outcomes (redpoint grade, injury incidence, pulley imaging, return-to-climbing time)

**Bibliography**
- Added 6 new verified entries: `Wangetal24`, `Boldt23`, `Balshaw23`, `Miyamoto25`, `Garrison20`, `Schulze24`
