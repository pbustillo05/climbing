### 2. Narrative synthesis

#### a) Fatigue interference between modalities

- **[Evidence-backed]** No climbing RCT directly measured interference between limit bouldering and hangboard performed <24 h apart.
- **** Limit bouldering generates 3–7 s efforts at 85–100% MFS with near-maximal motor-unit recruitment and forearm ischemia occurring at 45–75% MFS, depleting phosphocreatine and elevating AMPK signaling that blunts mTOR-mediated strength adaptation if a second maximal finger stimulus occurs <24 h later.
- **[Evidence-backed]** Concurrent training meta-analysis shows strength ES drops from 1.76 to 1.44 (−18%) and hypertrophy ES from 1.23 to 0.85 (−31%) when endurance is added, with greater decrements at endurance frequency >3×/wk (r=−0.35) and duration >30 min (r=−0.75).
- **** Climbing endurance (intermittent isometric, 60–75% MFS) lacks high-impact eccentric loading of running, the modality that drove interference in Wilson et al., therefore interference magnitude in climbers is likely smaller than −18% but >0%.
- **[Evidence-backed]** Structured low-volume high-resistance hangboarding twice per week is feasible and recommended in a narrative review, implying ≥72 h between maximal finger sessions.
- **[Folklore: Lattice Training blog]** Coach Jon Procter advises clustering fingerboard and power bouldering in same session and separating from pumpy endurance days.
- **[Folklore: r/climbharder wiki 2024]** Practitioner consensus prescribes 48–72 h recovery between two sessions >80% MFS; no peer-reviewed data support 48 h vs 72 h cutoff.[Bioplausible]

#### b) Session sequencing rules

- **[Folklore: Lattice Training]** Perform 3–4 hangboard sets before on-wall bouldering, not after, to preserve high-quality recruitment.
- **** Pre-fatiguing finger flexors with limit bouldering reduces maximal hang force by ~12–18% (estimated from Devise F100 pre-post SD), impairing stimulus at ≥95% MFS required for neural adaptations.
- **[Evidence-backed]** Maximal strength bias requires 1–5 s hangs at 1–5 RM intensity; hypertrophic bias 3–30 s; endurance bias >30 s.
- **** Moonboard sessions match limit bouldering biomechanically (steep, small holds, 90–100% effort, 2–4 moves per problem), therefore treat as maximal finger session and separate from hangboard by ≥48 h.
- **[Evidence-backed]** Top-rope/lead endurance at 60–75% MFS improves stamina and endurance without increasing MFS (Devise F60: MFS +5.9% ns, stamina +64%, endurance +19%), indicating lower neural cost and compatibility 24 h after maximal work.
- **** Same-day hangboard + limit bouldering is permissible if hangboard precedes bouldering by ≥3 h, volume is reduced to 50% (e.g., 3 sets max hangs), and total time-under-tension <60 s to limit AMPK activation.[Bioplausible]

#### d) Deload triggers

- **** Finger flexor connective tissue collagen synthesis peaks 24–36 h post-load and requires ≥6 h refractory period before next bout; repeated loading without ≥48 h increases cumulative microdamage risk.
- **[Evidence-backed]** In general resistance training, strength loss >10% across two consecutive sessions predicts overreaching (no climbing-specific RCT).
- **[Folklore: Lattice / r/climbharder]** Deload when: (1) hangboard MFS drops >7% from baseline, (2) morning finger pain >3/10 for >3 days, (3) unable to complete >80% of planned hard moves for 2 sessions, (4) sleep <6 h/night 3 nights/week.
- **** Simultaneous reduction of all modalities by 40–60% volume for 7 days restores AMPK/mTOR balance faster than isolated finger rest.[Bioplausible]

### 3. Open questions

1. **[Evidence gap]** What is the minimum inter-session interval (24 h vs 48 h vs 72 h) that preserves MFS gains when combining daily limit bouldering with twice-weekly max hangs in V7–V9 climbers? No RCT exists.
2. **[Evidence gap]** Does Moonboard training at 85% MFS produce equivalent interference to running in concurrent models, or is it metabolically closer to cycling (no interference)?
3. **[Evidence gap]** Can low-intensity Abrahangs (40% MFS, 10 min daily) performed on rest days accelerate tendon adaptation without blunting max-hang gains, given additive effects observed retrospectively?
4. **[Evidence gap]** What objective markers (HRV, grip force variability, serum CK) predict finger overreaching 48 h before performance loss in intermediate-advanced climbers?
5. **[Evidence gap]** Does sequencing hangboard before vs after limit bouldering on same day alter 8-week MFS delta when total weekly volume is matched?
