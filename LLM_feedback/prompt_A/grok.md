**2. Narrative synthesis**

**a) Fatigue interference between modalities**  
**[Evidence-backed: Stien et al. (2023)]** Resistance training (incl. hangboard) added to regular climbing improved finger strength (SDM = 0.41, 95% CI 0.03–0.80) and climbing performance (SDM = 0.57) vs climbing alone across 4–10 wk interventions; no studies isolated interference from limit bouldering. **[Bioplausible: AMPK/mTOR conflict from general concurrent-training literature (Hickson 1980; Wilson et al. 2012 meta-analysis); no climbing-specific RCT]** Limit bouldering (near-maximal dynamic finger loading + high CNS demand) and hangboard (pure isometric maximal loading) both drive mTOR-mediated hypertrophy/strength pathways; concurrent endurance climbing (metabolic demand) activates AMPK, which can blunt mTOR signaling if sessions are <4–8 h apart or volume exceeds recovery capacity. **[Bioplausible: tendon recovery kinetics; no finger-flexor RCT]** Finger flexors (flexor digitorum profundus/superficialis) exhibit slower tendon adaptation than muscle (72 h peak collagen synthesis); high-intensity finger loading (>80% MFS) within <48 h risks cumulative strain without direct evidence. **[Folklore: Lattice Training blog (2023); TrainingBeta podcast]** Practitioner consensus states high-intensity finger sessions (hangboard or limit bouldering) within <48 h in the same microcycle reduce adaptation and increase overuse risk; no mechanism or controlled data cited. **[Evidence-backed: Hickson (1980)]** Concurrent strength + endurance reduced strength gains after 7 wk vs strength-only; applicability to climbing’s isometric/dynamic profile remains untested in RCTs. Minimum recovery: **[Evidence-backed: general concurrent literature (Wilson et al. 2012 meta; recent reviews)]** 4–8 h between sessions eliminates acute interference on strength-endurance performance; **[Bioplausible]** chronic finger-tendon recovery likely requires ≥48 h given loading profile; **[Folklore: r/climbharder; Lattice Training]** ≥48 h between high-intensity finger sessions is standard heuristic.

**b) Session sequencing rules**  
**[Bioplausible: CNS demand hierarchy; no climbing RCT]** Hangboard (pure maximal isometric, low movement complexity) should precede limit bouldering/projecting (high CNS + skill demand) on the same day to preserve neuromuscular recruitment for technique; rationale is pre-fatigue from bouldering impairs hangboard force output. **[Folklore: Lattice Training blog (2023)]** Hangboard precedes strength/power bouldering in same-day templates; reverse order risks technique degradation. **[Bioplausible: specificity of Moonboard loading]** Moonboard sessions (system-board, high-intensity dynamic finger loading, benchmark problems) share fatigue profile with limit bouldering (≥80% MFS, high RFD demand) and should be treated as equivalent high-intensity finger days; place on separate days from hangboard or ≥48 h apart. **[Bioplausible: metabolic vs neural fatigue]** Top-rope/lead endurance sessions (submaximal, 60–75% effort, sustained contraction) produce primarily metabolic (lactate, forearm pump) rather than neural fatigue and can follow or precede strength sessions with minimal interference if volume is controlled; relative to bouldering/hangboarding they impose lower peak tendon load but higher cumulative ischemia. **[Evidence-backed: López-Rivera & González-Badillo (2019)]** Hangboard + climbing same day (hangs first + 15 min rest before 1.5–2 h climbing) produced endurance gains without reported interference in the intervention arm. **[Bioplausible]** Hangboard and limit bouldering can be done same day if ≥3–4 h gap (to clear acute neural/metabolic fatigue) and hangboard performed first; total daily finger loading must stay <120 min high-intensity equivalent; no RCT confirms long-term adaptation superiority vs separate days.

**c) Example weekly structures**  
All templates assume 4–5 training days/wk for V6–V9 climbers; intensity descriptors use % MFS or effort; volumes are approximate. **[Folklore-derived: Lattice Training blog (2023); TrainingBeta; r/climbharder wiki]** No evidence-backed weekly template exists; structures below synthesize common practitioner practice.  

- **Template 1 (4 days, strength-priority; folklore-derived)**  
  Mon: Hangboard max-hangs (85–90% MFS, 3–5 × 5–10 s, 20–30 min total) **[Bioplausible: Devise et al. (2022) intensity specificity]**  
  Tue: Rest or active recovery (technique laps, <60% effort, 45 min)  
  Wed: Limit bouldering projecting (90–100% effort, 2–2.5 h, 20–30 attempts) **[Folklore: r/climbharder]**  
  Thu: Rest  
  Fri: Moonboard session (benchmark problems, 70–85% effort, 90 min) **[Folklore: Moonboard training guides]**  
  Sat: Top-rope/lead endurance (routes 60–75% max, 2 h, 4–6 climbs) **[Bioplausible]**  
  Sun: Rest  

- **Template 2 (5 days, balanced; folklore-derived)**  
  Mon: Hangboard (max or repeaters, 20 min) + 3–4 h later limit bouldering (90–100% effort, 1.5 h) **[Folklore: Lattice Training same-day sequencing]**  
  Tue: Active recovery / technique (submaximal, 60 min)  
  Wed: Top-rope/lead endurance (60–75% effort, 2 h)  
  Thu: Moonboard (70–85% effort, 90 min)  
  Fri: Rest  
  Sat: Limit bouldering or projecting (90–100% effort, 2 h)  
  Sun: Rest or light endurance  

- **Template 3 (4 days, endurance-priority for sport climbers; folklore-derived)**  
  Mon: Hangboard max-hangs (85% MFS, 20 min)  
  Tue: Top-rope/lead endurance (60–75% effort, 2.5 h) **[Bioplausible: low interference if ≥24 h from hangboard]**  
  Wed: Rest  
  Thu: Limit bouldering (90–100% effort, 2 h)  
  Fri: Moonboard or power endurance (70–85% effort, 90 min)  
  Sat: Rest  
  Sun: Optional light technique  

**d) Deload triggers**  
**[Folklore: Lattice Training; r/climbharder; TrainingBeta coaching heuristics]** Reduce volume/intensity 40–60% across all modalities every 4–6 wk or when any two of the following occur simultaneously: (1) ≥10% drop in session performance (e.g., max hang load or boulder sends), (2) persistent forearm soreness >48 h post-session, (3) elevated subjective readiness score (e.g., >5/10 on fatigue scales), (4) stalled progress on benchmark problems ≥2 consecutive weeks. **[Bioplausible: overreaching markers from general training literature]** No climbing-specific RCT validates these markers; they predict overreaching before overtraining via cumulative fatigue monitoring but lack sensitivity/specificity data in finger-flexor loading.

**3. Open questions**  
1. What is the minimum inter-session recovery interval (hours or days) between hangboard and limit bouldering that preserves long-term finger-strength adaptation in a controlled RCT?  
2. Does same-day sequencing (hangboard first vs bouldering first) produce measurable differences in 8–12 wk finger-strength or climbing-performance gains?  
3. How does Moonboard volume interact with hangboard frequency in a periodized microcycle—does equated total high-intensity finger loading (>80% MFS) blunt gains regardless of modality split?  
4. Are subjective readiness markers (soreness, performance drop) or objective biomarkers (e.g., finger-flexor critical force) predictive of overreaching in climbers undergoing concurrent modalities?  
5. What is the effect size of concurrent top-rope/lead endurance on hangboard-derived maximal finger strength when sessions are separated by ≥24 h vs same-day?
