## 2. Narrative synthesis

## a) Fatigue interference between modalities

**[Evidence-backed]** Devise et al. showed that maximal-intensity hangboard work (F100) increased MFS over 4 wk, while submaximal repeated efforts (F60/F80) preferentially improved stamina/endurance, indicating session stimulus specificity. If a climber performs a glycolytic/endurance finger session immediately before a maximal-strength session, acute local forearm fatigue likely reduces force output and degrades the intended strength stimulus. Direct same-day sequencing was **not** tested. ([Frontiers][3])

**[Bioplausible]** Limit bouldering and Moonboard climbing both impose near-maximal digital flexor force, high motor-unit recruitment, and connective-tissue strain on A2/A4 pulley systems and FDP/FDS tendons. Performing hangboard max hangs within ≤24 h of such sessions likely lowers peak force capacity through residual neural fatigue, tendon stiffness perturbation, and finger flexor metabolite accumulation. Direct RCT evidence in climbers is absent.

**[Evidence-backed]** Non-climbing concurrent-training literature shows endurance work can interfere with strength gains when performed frequently, with high volume, or close in time to strength sessions. Transfer to climbing is partial because climbing endurance sessions are more isometric and forearm-localized than running/cycling, but the core principle—competing acute fatigue and signaling—likely still applies.

**[Folklore: Lattice Training blog, r/climbharder recurring advice]** Separate “hard finger sessions” by **48 h**. This heuristic is widely used because connective tissue soreness and skin recovery often lag muscle recovery. No controlled climbing trial validates 48 h as an optimum.

**[Bioplausible]** Minimum practical spacing hierarchy for intermediate–advanced climbers:

* Same tissue/system repeated maximally (Moonboard → max hangs): **24–48 h** preferable.
* Max hangs → endurance routes: **12–24 h** often tolerable.
* Endurance routes → max hangs: **24 h** preferable if forearm pump was severe.

## b) Session sequencing rules

**[Evidence-backed]** If the goal is maximal finger-force adaptation, place hangboard max-strength work **before** fatigue-inducing climbing or on a separate day, because strength adaptations depend on high force outputs and quality repetitions. Devise’s F100 protocol supports high-intensity force-specific adaptation. ([Frontiers][3])

**[Bioplausible]** If the goal is movement skill/projecting, place limit bouldering first because route-reading, coordination, and maximal attempt quality decline under pre-fatigue.

**[Folklore: performance coaches / common practice]** Many advanced climbers alternate blocks: project first during outdoor season, hangboard first during strength blocks.

**[Bioplausible]** Moonboard sessions should be treated as another high-intensity finger session, not a “technique day.” Benchmark board climbing often includes small edges, high contact force, and repeated explosive pulling. Therefore place Moonboard ≥24–48 h from max hangs or limit bouldering when possible.

**[Bioplausible]** Top-rope/lead endurance sessions generate lower instantaneous finger force than Moonboard/limit bouldering but higher cumulative forearm ischemia time and metabolite stress. They are less disruptive to CNS freshness but can impair next-day finger-force output if done to repeated pump/failure.

**[Evidence-backed + Bioplausible]** Can hangboard and limit bouldering occur same day? Yes, but objective determines order:

* Strength priority: hangboard first, then limit bouldering reduced volume.
* Skill/project priority: bouldering first, then short hangboard maintenance.
  Use **≥20–30 min** passive recovery between segments if same session; direct evidence absent, but enough time may partially restore phosphocreatine.

## c) Example weekly structures (4–5 d/wk)

### Template 1 — Strength-emphasis off-season (4 d/wk)

* **Mon:** Hangboard max hangs, 85–100% MFS, 5–8 total work sets, 20–30 min. **[Evidence-backed: Devise 2022 F100/F80]** ([Frontiers][3])
* **Tue:** Rest / mobility. **[Bioplausible]**
* **Wed:** Limit bouldering projecting, 2–3 h, 90–100% effort, long rests. **[Folklore: coaching consensus]**
* **Thu:** Easy aerobic / technique climbing, 60 min, submaximal. **[Bioplausible]**
* **Fri:** Moonboard benchmarks, 75–90 min, high intensity, capped volume. **[Folklore: Moonboard practice norms]**
* **Sat:** Rest.
* **Sun:** Lead mileage, 90 min at 60–75% max. **[Bioplausible]**

### Template 2 — Route-performance emphasis (5 d/wk)

* **Mon:** Lead intervals / linked routes, 4–8 burns, moderate pump. **[Bioplausible]**
* **Tue:** Rest.
* **Wed:** Hangboard max hangs maintenance, 4–6 sets. **[Evidence-backed]**
* **Thu:** Technique/top-rope mileage, easy volume. **[Bioplausible]**
* **Fri:** Limit bouldering 2 h. **[Folklore]**
* **Sat:** Redpoint attempts outdoors/indoors. **[Folklore]**
* **Sun:** Rest.

### Template 3 — Time-crunched mixed plan (4 d/wk)

* **Mon:** Combined session: limit bouldering first (75 min) + short max hangs second (3–4 sets). **[Bioplausible]**
* **Wed:** Endurance routes / 4x4s. **[Folklore: common climbing conditioning]**
* **Fri:** Moonboard power session. **[Folklore]**
* **Sun:** Easy mileage / movement drills. **[Bioplausible]**

**Constraint across templates:** Keep total “hard finger exposures” (limit bouldering, Moonboard, max hangs) at **2–3/wk** for most V6–V9 climbers unless recovery capacity is proven. **[Folklore: widespread coaching heuristic]**

## d) Deload triggers

**[Evidence-backed]** In strength literature, planned deloads every **3–6 wk** are common when performance stalls or fatigue accumulates; climbing-specific RCTs are lacking.

**[Bioplausible]** Reduce volume/intensity across all modalities simultaneously when ≥2 markers persist for **≥7 days**:

* MFS / hang load down **>5%** from normal on two sessions.
* Warm-up grades feel ≥1 full grade harder.
* Finger soreness on first crimp persists >24 h.
* Sleep disruption / elevated irritability / reduced motivation.
* Skin or pulley irritation altering grip choice.

**[Folklore: coach monitoring practice]** If Moonboard benchmark send rate halves for 2 consecutive weeks at same effort, deload.

**[Bioplausible]** Effective deload: reduce total volume **30–50%**, keep intensity moderate-high on 1 weekly exposure to retain neural qualities, avoid repeated failure pumps, maintain easy movement sessions.

## 3. Open questions

1. **No direct evidence** compares **48 h vs 72 h vs autoregulated spacing** between hard finger sessions in trained climbers.
2. **No RCT** tests whether hangboard-first vs limit-bouldering-first sequencing yields superior long-term gains in V6–V9 athletes.
3. **Unknown dose-response** for combining Moonboard + hangboard in one microcycle without tendon overuse risk.
4. **No validated readiness metric** exists for climbers analogous jump-height or bar-velocity monitoring; daily MFS testing may itself add fatigue.
5. **Sex differences, age effects, and tissue tolerance** are under-studied; current protocols are male-skewed and small-sample.
