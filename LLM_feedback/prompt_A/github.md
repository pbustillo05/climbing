## 2) Narrative synthesis

### a) Fatigue interference between modalities

**[Evidence-backed]** **Climbing-specific RCTs support that adding high-intensity finger-specific training (hangboard/campus-style) on top of “normal climbing” can improve finger-strength proxies over 4–8 weeks**, implying that at least some microcycle combinations are tolerable in trained climbers, but **these studies do not experimentally vary session spacing/order**, so they do **not** directly answer “how close is too close” within a 7‑day plan. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8241953/?utm_source=openai))

**[Bioplausible]** **Limit bouldering + Moonboard sessions and max-hang hangboarding all concentrate near-maximal finger flexor isometric force, high motor-unit recruitment, and high local forearm fatigue**, so placing them <24 h apart plausibly reduces “quality reps” (force-time integral at high %MFS) via residual peripheral fatigue (metabolite accumulation, reduced force capacity) and central fatigue (reduced voluntary drive), but **direct climbing RCT evidence on same-week sequencing is absent**. (Mechanism: acute force suppression post high-intensity isometrics; gap: no RCT randomizing session order/spacing with finger outcomes.)

**[Evidence-backed]** **General concurrent-training evidence shows endurance training can attenuate strength/power outcomes when combined in the same training period**, with interference moderated by endurance frequency/duration/modality; this supports treating **high-volume route endurance** as a possible interferer with maximal strength development if dose is high. ([uptodate.com](https://www.uptodate.com/contents/practical-guidelines-for-implementing-a-strength-training-program-for-adults/abstract-text/22002517/pubmed?utm_source=openai))

**[Bioplausible]** **Applicability of classic concurrent-training interference to climbing is incomplete** because (i) climbing “endurance” is often intermittent isometric forearm work with occlusion-like blood-flow restriction patterns, and (ii) climbing endurance sessions can still contain high-force crux attempts; thus “endurance day” can unintentionally become mixed high-intensity exposure, which makes interference highly dose- and route-selection-dependent. (Mechanism: AMPK–mTOR signaling competition is often cited in concurrent literature; gap: not validated for forearm flexors under climbing-specific ischemic patterns.)

**[Folklore: Lattice Training / common coaching heuristics]** **Minimum recovery between finger-max sessions is commonly prescribed as 48 h** (e.g., between max-hangs, Moonboard limit sessions, and hard projecting), with **24 h used only for low-intensity technique volume**, but this is **not derived from controlled sequencing trials in climbers**. (Mechanism often stated: restore neural output + connective-tissue tolerance; gap: no RCT.)

**[Evidence-backed]** **A climbing-specific recovery study supports that “easy climbing” can function as an active recovery mode versus non-specific recovery (walking) for subsequent climbing performance/fatigue markers**, supporting placement of technique/easy mileage on days between hard finger sessions when the goal is to preserve next-day quality. ([pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26664273/?utm_source=openai))

### b) Session sequencing rules

**[Bioplausible]** **If the session goal is maximal finger-force exposure (≥90–100% effort), schedule the highest-skill, highest-intent task first (limit bouldering / Moonboard benchmarks) and place hangboard after only if it can be executed at target intensity (e.g., ≥80–100% MFS) without form breakdown**, because fatigue reduces force output and increases “compensations” (open-hand collapse, shoulder elevation), which can reduce specific stimulus and plausibly increase injury risk; **gap: no RCT comparing “hangboard→board” vs “board→hangboard” sequencing.**

**[Evidence-backed]** **Hangboard training performed at defined relative intensities (e.g., 60/80/100% MFS) can improve finger capabilities over 4 weeks**, implying that **hitting the intended intensity zone is a key control variable**; sequencing should prioritize maintaining that target intensity. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9039162/?utm_source=openai))

**[Evidence-backed]** **Added-weight hangboard protocols improved grip-strength proxies vs normal climbing in an 8‑week RCT**, supporting hangboard as a distinct stimulus rather than redundant with climbing alone; thus, sequencing should avoid turning hangboard into “junk volume” performed under heavy fatigue. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8241953/?utm_source=openai))

**[Bioplausible]** **Moonboard sessions should be treated as “high-intensity finger sessions” equivalent to limit bouldering for scheduling purposes** because they concentrate small-hold loading, high attempts near limit, and high repetition of similar grip positions; therefore, pairing Moonboard with max-hangs in the same 24 h window likely increases local tissue load density beyond what most intermediate–advanced athletes can recover from without reducing quality. (Gap: no Moonboard-specific intervention trials.)

**[Bioplausible]** **Top-rope/lead endurance sessions are typically lower peak finger force per move but higher cumulative time-under-tension and higher systemic load (ventilatory + trunk/hip) than bouldering**, so they can be placed (i) after ≥24 h from max finger sessions if kept submaximal (e.g., 60–75% redpoint intensity), or (ii) on the same day *after* max finger work if volume is capped to avoid converting it into a second high-intensity finger session. (Gap: little RCT evidence on weekly ordering.)

**[Folklore: r/climbharder + common hangboard guides]** **Same-day “limit bouldering + hangboard” is often used when total weekly availability is low**, with **order = limit bouldering first, hangboard second**, and **inter-session gap = 15–60 min** (snack + full rest) to preserve attempt quality; this is coaching consensus rather than controlled evidence. (Mechanism claimed: prioritize skill/coordination while fresh.)

**[Evidence-backed]** **High-frequency high-intensity finger-power training dose can matter**: campus board RCTs comparing 2×/wk vs 4��/wk dosing exist, implying that “more sessions” is a manipulable lever, but these studies do not map directly onto Moonboard+hangboard integration without additional assumptions. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8256519/?utm_source=openai))

### c) Example weekly structures (4–5 training days/week)

#### Template 1 — “2 hard finger days + 1 endurance day” (conservative integration)
- **Mon:** Hangboard max strength (F100-style: short efforts at ~100% MFS; 20–30 min total work; long rests) **[Evidence-backed]** (hangboard intensity specificity improves MFS over 4 weeks) ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9039162/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Tue:** Rest or easy technique (≤60% effort; 45–75 min; focus footwork) **[Bioplausible]** (low-load practice minimizes interference, preserves next-day force capacity; direct sequencing RCT absent).  
- **Wed:** Limit bouldering / projecting (90–100% effort; 2–3 h; ≤10–20 true limit attempts) **[Folklore: common coaching practice]** (no RCT on weekly placement).  
- **Thu:** Rest or active recovery (easy climbing vs walking if recovering between bouts) **[Evidence-backed]** (active recovery study supports easy climbing as recovery modality) ([pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26664273/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Fri:** Moonboard (benchmark problems; 80–95% effort; 75–120 min; stop when move quality drops) **[Bioplausible]** (treated as high-intensity finger session; no Moonboard RCTs).  
- **Sat:** Top-rope/lead endurance (routes at ~60–75% max; 90–150 min; avoid cruxy near-limit tries) **[Bioplausible]** (concurrent interference logic suggests controlling endurance dose) ([uptodate.com](https://www.uptodate.com/contents/practical-guidelines-for-implementing-a-strength-training-program-for-adults/abstract-text/22002517/pubmed?utm_source=openai))  
- **Sun:** Rest **[Folklore: standard microcycle practice]**.

#### Template 2 — “Same-day stacking to protect rest days” (time-efficient, higher density)
- **Mon:** Limit bouldering first (90–100% effort; 2–2.5 h) **[Folklore: coaching heuristic]**.  
  - **+ 30–60 min later:** Hangboard repeaters or F80-style intermittent (target ~80% MFS; 15–25 min work) **[Evidence-backed]** (80% MFS protocols improve multiple finger capabilities over 4 weeks) ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9039162/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Tue:** Rest **[Folklore]**.  
- **Wed:** Top-rope/lead endurance (60–75% effort; 2 h) **[Bioplausible]** (manage endurance dose to reduce interference risk) ([uptodate.com](https://www.uptodate.com/contents/practical-guidelines-for-implementing-a-strength-training-program-for-adults/abstract-text/22002517/pubmed?utm_source=openai))  
- **Thu:** Rest or easy technique **[Bioplausible]**.  
- **Fri:** Moonboard power-endurance emphasis (70–85% effort; density circuits; 75–90 min) **[Folklore: Moonboard community programming]**.  
- **Sat:** Optional easy volume (ARC-style continuous easy climbing 30–45 min) **[Bioplausible]** (aerobic forearm adaptation rationale; direct RCT integration absent).  
- **Sun:** Rest **[Folklore]**.

#### Template 3 — “Hangboard priority block + reduced board intensity” (strength-biased mesocycle)
- **Mon:** Hangboard added-weight progression (e.g., +1–1.25 kg weekly; 6–10 s hangs; long rests; 25–35 min) **[Evidence-backed]** (added-weight hangboard improved grip strength vs control in 8-week RCT) ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8241953/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Tue:** Easy climbing / technique (≤60% effort; 60–90 min) **[Bioplausible]**.  
- **Wed:** Moonboard but cap intensity (70–85% effort; 60–90 min; no all-out max attempts) **[Bioplausible]** (reduce interference with Mon strength focus; no direct data).  
- **Thu:** Rest **[Folklore]**.  
- **Fri:** Hangboard F80 intermittent (stamina/endurance emphasis; 20–30 min) **[Evidence-backed]** (F80 improves adjacent capacities in 4-week RCT) ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9039162/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Sat:** Lead endurance (60–70% effort; 90–120 min) **[Bioplausible]**.  
- **Sun:** Rest **[Folklore]**.

**[Evidence-backed]** **None of the above weekly templates are directly “evidence-derived” as complete 7‑day schedules**, because the climbing hangboard/campus RCTs manipulate training modality and frequency but typically **do not randomize full weekly integration across multiple modalities**; the templates are therefore **hybrid**: evidence for component efficacy + bioplausible/foklore for weekly placement. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8241953/?utm_source=openai))

### d) Deload triggers

**[Evidence-backed]** **Higher-frequency finger-power work can be a stressor that likely increases the need for deloading in some athletes**, supported indirectly by the existence of dosing comparisons (e.g., campus 2× vs 4×/wk) and by component studies showing measurable adaptation in short blocks (4–8 weeks), which is a time window where functional overreaching is plausible if load continues to rise without recovery; however, **climbing RCTs rarely report overreaching markers**, so trigger thresholds remain uncertain. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8256519/?utm_source=openai))

**[Bioplausible]** Deload across **all modalities simultaneously** is mechanistically justified when **performance decrements persist ≥7 days despite ≥2 rest days**, because persistent decrements imply accumulated neuromuscular/connective-tissue fatigue rather than single-session soreness; **gap: no validated climbing-specific readiness algorithm.**

**[Folklore: coaching practice]** Common deload heuristic is **reduce total volume by ~30–50% for 5–7 days** while keeping **some intensity exposures** (e.g., 1 short max-hang session at reduced sets) to preserve neural qualities; this is widely used in climbing programming but **not tested in controlled deload trials**.

**[Bioplausible]** Practical markers that plausibly precede non-functional overreaching in climbers include: (i) **≥5–10% drop in max-hang load or measured MFS** across ≥2 sessions, (ii) **increased finger pain at PIP/A2 region at submax loads**, and (iii) **inability to reproduce benchmark Moonboard moves at usual warm-up time**, but **predictive validity is unproven** (gap: prospective cohort prediction studies lacking).

## 3) Open questions (literature gaps)

1) **[Evidence-backed]** No RCTs directly compare **session order** (hangboard→limit bouldering vs limit bouldering→hangboard) while controlling intensity and total weekly load and measuring both **finger strength proxies and climbing performance** (e.g., redpoint grade or standardized route). ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8241953/?utm_source=openai))  
2) **[Evidence-backed]** No controlled trials define **minimum effective recovery interval** (e.g., 24 h vs 48 h vs 72 h) between two **high-intensity finger sessions** using objective outcomes (MFS, RFD, climbing performance). ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9039162/?utm_source=openai))  
3) **[Evidence-backed]** Limited evidence quantifies whether adding high-volume lead endurance causes a measurable **interference effect** on finger strength/hypertrophy in climbers specifically, beyond what is inferred from general concurrent-training meta-analyses. ([uptodate.com](https://www.uptodate.com/contents/practical-guidelines-for-implementing-a-strength-training-program-for-adults/abstract-text/22002517/pubmed?utm_source=openai))  
4) **[Evidence-backed]** Transfer is under-measured: many finger interventions improve **lab/proxy outcomes** (MFS, pinch strength, RFD) without simultaneously testing **ecologically valid climbing performance endpoints** (IRCRA change, redpoint/onsight changes) under standardized conditions. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9039162/?utm_source=openai))  
5) **[Evidence-backed]** There is no robust evidence defining how to integrate **system board training (Moonboard-specific)** with hangboard protocols, because Moonboard interventions are not isolated as a modality in controlled trials (existing board studies focus more on campus/fingerboard paradigms). ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8256519/?utm_source=openai))
