Recovery for rock climbers is constrained by two different clocks: forearm muscle recovers in 24–72 h, finger flexor tendon and pulley collagen turns over in days to weeks.

## 2. Narrative synthesis

### Sub-section 1: Sleep
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Sleep extension improves athletic performance measures relevant to climbing (reaction time, power). Mah 2011 showed +110.9 min sleep increased sprint speed 4.3% and reduced PVT mean reaction time by 36.3 ms (p=0.004). Outcome is functional (sprint, shooting) and surrogate cognitive (PVT).
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Acute sleep deprivation reduces muscle protein synthesis by 18% in humans (Saner et al. 2020, DOI 10.1096/fj.202000230R) – surrogate metabolic outcome. Direct climbing evidence absent.
- ****: Slow-wave sleep drives ~70% of daily growth hormone pulses. GH stimulates hepatic IGF-1, which upregulates tendon fibroblast collagen I synthesis. No RCT has linked sleep loss to reduced tendon collagen synthesis in climbers; mechanistic gap remains.
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Halson 2014 review concludes elite athletes averaging <7 h show impaired reaction time, strength, and endurance.
- Practical recommendations:
  - Duration 8–10 h time-in-bed **[Folklore: US Olympic Committee sleep guidelines, also supported by Mah data]**
  - Consistent wake time ±30 min, cool dark room, limit caffeine >6 h pre-sleep **[Evidence-backed for sleep hygiene generally, not climbing-specific]**[Bioplausible]

### Sub-section 2: Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Monitoring
- HRV = beat-to-beat R-R interval variation, primarily RMSSD reflecting cardiac vagal tone. **[Evidence-backed]**
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Kiviniemi 2007 RCT: HRV-guided training produced greater Loadmax improvement than fixed schedule (Δ +0.9 vs +0.5 km/h, p=0.048). Functional outcome.
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Systematic review of 8 studies (2023) found HRV-guided training improves VO2max and Wmax vs predefined (meta-analysis cited in search) – functional.
- HRV thresholds: "ready" often defined as daily RMSSD within 0.5 SD of 7-day rolling mean, or >1 SD drop = reduce intensity. **[Folklore: Plews, Buchheit, HRV4Training documentation]** – no universal RCT-validated cutoff.
- Tools validation:
  - Oura Gen3/4: CCC 0.97–0.99 vs ECG for nocturnal RMSSD, MAPE 5.96–7.15% **[Evidence-backed]**
  - WHOOP 4.0: CCC 0.94, MAPE 8.17% – moderate accuracy **[Evidence-backed]**
  - Garmin Fenix 6: CCC 0.87, MAPE 10.5% – poor **[Evidence-backed]**
- ****: HRV captures autonomic recovery, not local tendon matrix turnover. Tendon pain and collagen synthesis lag HRV by days; HRV cannot directly index pulley readiness. Gap: no study correlates HRV with finger flexor tendon stiffness or injury risk.[Bioplausible]

### Sub-section 3: Contrast Therapy
- Mechanism: alternating 10–15°C cold / 38–40°C hot causes vasoconstriction-vasodilation pumping, hypothesized to enhance metabolite clearance and reduce edema. ****
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Dupuy meta-analysis: CWT reduces DOMS small effect SMD -0.40 (95% CI -0.73 to -0.07), no effect on perceived fatigue SMD -0.04 (-0.86 to 0.79). Outcomes surrogate (pain scores).
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Bieuzen 2013 systematic review (cited in Dupuy) found CWT reduced pain at 24–72 h post-eccentric exercise. Functional performance recovery inconsistent.
- Connective tissue: **** only – no RCT shows CWT accelerates tendon collagen synthesis. Animal data suggest cold reduces tenocyte metabolism; heat may increase blood flow but evidence lacking.
- Cold-water immersion separately:
  - **[Evidence-backed]**: Roberts 2015: 12-wk CWI post-strength blunted type II fibre hypertrophy (+0% vs +17% ACT) and strength gains. Surrogate (CSA) + functional (strength).
  - Mechanism: blunted p70S6K phosphorylation and satellite cell expansion up to 48 h.
  - For climbers doing hangboard (high mechanical tension), regular CWI likely impairs finger flexor hypertrophy adaptations. **[Bioplausible extrapolation]**
- Practical protocol if used for soreness only (not after strength): 3–4 cycles of 1 min cold (10–15°C) / 1–2 min warm (38–40°C), total 12–15 min, within 2 h post-session, ≤2×/week. **[Folklore: Australian Institute of Sport recovery guidelines]**[Bioplausible]

### Sub-section 4: Soft-Tissue Work
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Molouki 2016 RCT: single 5-min forearm massage increased grip endurance 38.4→46.5 s (+21%, p<0.001), no significant strength change. Outcome functional surrogate for climbing endurance.
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Dupuy meta: massage most effective for DOMS (SMD ~ -0.85 large) and fatigue, across 99 studies.
- Timing: immediate post-exercise massage shows acute endurance benefit; next-day effects on DOMS peak at 24–48 h. No difference shown for climbing-specific timing – **** that immediate improves blood flow, delayed reduces soreness.
- Climbing-specific tools (forearm roller, lacrosse ball finger flexor release, "voodoo floss"): **[Folklore: Lattice Training, Dave MacLeod coaching blogs]** – widely advocated, no controlled trials.[Bioplausible]

### Sub-section 5: Active Recovery
- **[Evidence-backed]**: Dupuy meta: active recovery reduces DOMS with moderate effect, superior to passive rest, but smaller than massage. SMD range -0.4 to -1.0 depending on intensity.
- Mechanism: low-intensity contraction (30–40% VO2max) increases forearm blood flow 2–3×, accelerates lactate clearance. **[Evidence-backed]** from cycling studies, extrapolated to forearm.
- For climbers: "active recovery" defined as:
  - 15–20 min easy traversing on large holds (<40% max effort), or
  - antagonist training (push-ups, reverse wrist curls), or
  - low-intensity cycling/rowing to maintain systemic circulation.
  **[Folklore: Anderson brothers' Rock Climber's Training Manual]**, supported mechanistically by blood flow data ****.[Bioplausible]

### Sub-section 6: Nutrition Timing for Connective Tissue
- Muscle protein synthesis: **[Evidence-backed]** 20–40 g high-leucine protein (whey 0.3 g/kg) within 0–2 h post-exercise maximizes MPS. Surrogate outcome.
- Connective tissue-specific:
  - **[Evidence-backed]**: Shaw 2017: 15 g gelatin + 48 mg vitamin C taken 1 h before 6-min rope skipping doubled serum PINP (collagen synthesis marker) vs placebo. Engineered ligaments treated with post-supplement serum showed increased collagen content.
  - Dose: 15 g hydrolyzed collagen (or gelatin) + 50 mg vitamin C, 30–60 min pre-loading session. Timing matters because exercise-induced blood flow delivers proline/glycine to tendon during mechanotransduction window.
  - Muscle vs tendon: MPS peaks post-exercise; collagen synthesis peaks with pre-exercise amino acid availability + loading. **[Bioplausible mechanism from Shaw]**
- Energy availability: **[Evidence-backed]** low energy availability (<30 kcal/kg FFM/day) impairs tendon collagen synthesis and increases injury risk (Mountjoy RED-S consensus). No climbing-specific RCT, but mechanistic data from endurance athletes apply.

## 3. Practical recovery protocol (4–5 days/week climbing)

- Sleep target 8.5–9.5 h time-in-bed, consistent wake ±30 min. **[Evidence-backed for performance; Folklore for exact duration]**
- HRV use: yes, daily morning RMSSD via validated device (Oura or WHOOP). If RMSSD drops >1 SD below 7-day mean for 2 consecutive days, reduce hangboard intensity 50% or switch to technique day. **[Bioplausible + Folklore: threshold not RCT-validated for tendon]**
- Contrast therapy: avoid after finger strength sessions. Use only after high-volume pump sessions, ≤2×/week, protocol above. **[Evidence-backed for DOMS, Bioplausible for tendon]**
- Soft-tissue work: 5-min forearm effleurage + kneading immediately post-session (as per Molouki) to preserve grip endurance. Add 10-min next-day self-massage for DOMS. **[Evidence-backed]**
- Active recovery: 20-min easy traversing or cycling at <120 bpm on rest days. **[Evidence-backed for DOMS, Bioplausible for forearm]**
- Nutrition timing:
  - Pre-session (60 min before): 15 g collagen peptides + 50 mg vitamin C + ~30 g carbs. **[Evidence-backed for collagen]**
  - Post-session (within 2 h): 0.3 g/kg whey protein + carbs to replenish glycogen. **[Evidence-backed for muscle]**
  - Maintain energy availability >40 kcal/kg FFM/day. **[Evidence-backed]**

Label summary:
- Sleep: Evidence-backed
- HRV monitoring: Evidence-backed for autonomic, Bioplausible for tendon
- CWI avoidance post-strength: Evidence-backed
- CWT for soreness: Evidence-backed (small effect)
- Massage: Evidence-backed
- Pre-exercise collagen: Evidence-backed
- Active recovery specifics for climbing: Folklore

## 4. Open questions

1. Does daily HRV predict finger pulley injury risk better than session RPE in climbers training >12 h/week? No longitudinal data.
2. What is the optimal collagen dose-timing for finger flexor tendon adaptation under high-intensity isometric loading (hangboard) vs dynamic climbing?
3. Does regular CWT impair long-term finger strength gains similarly to CWI, given its alternating heat component?
4. Can soft-tissue work accelerate pulley collagen remodeling, or does it only modulate pain perception?
5. What sleep architecture (slow-wave sleep minutes) threshold correlates with serum PINP response to pre-exercise collagen in climbers?
